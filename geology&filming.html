Geological History

Ancient Rock Formations
The region is renowned for its diverse rock formations, primarily consisting of sedimentary rocks like limestone, sandstone, and shale. The limestone formations are particularly notable, having formed during the Cambrian period. These rocks have been shaped by millions of years of geological processes, including tectonic movements, weathering, and erosion, creating stunning landscapes such as caves, cliffs, and unique rock outcrops.

Geopark Status
In recognition of its significant geological heritage, Langkawi was declared a UNESCO Global Geopark in 2007, the first in Southeast Asia. Pantai Kok and its surrounding areas are integral parts of this geopark, showcasing a fascinating array of geological features. Filming History

Notable Productions
Several international and local productions have chosen Pantai Kok and its surroundings as filming locations. One of the most famous movies filmed in Langkawi is the 1999 Hollywood film "Anna and the King," starring Jodie Foster and Chow Yun-Fat. The film utilized various locations around Langkawi, including the beautiful beaches and lush landscapes of Pantai Kok, to depict 19th-century Siam.

Popular TV Shows
In addition to movies, Pantai Kok has also been featured in numerous travel and adventure television shows. These shows highlight the natural beauty and unique attractions of the area, bringing global attention to this serene destination.

Promotional Campaigns
Pantai Kok and Langkawi are often featured in promotional campaigns for tourism. These campaigns showcase the idyllic scenery and rich cultural heritage, attracting visitors from around the world. The area's visual appeal makes it an excellent choice for commercials and travel documentaries, further cementing its status as a must-visit destination.
